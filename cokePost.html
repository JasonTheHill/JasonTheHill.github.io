Thin Cardboard for 24 pack soda boxes?

It used to be that once upon a time, soda boxes (especially the larger ones) were made with thick cardboard that was difficult to puncture, tear, and was resistant to being crushed. While this made it a bit annoying to get to the sodas, I never had to worry about the integrity of the box as I carried it to the car and transported it home. These days, the cardboard on those larger soda boxes seems to be MUCH thinner as a method to reduce production costs. The result? If I am not careful when carrying the case of soda by its intended handle, it will rip without warning and drop heavy cans of Coke on my foot and leg. This has happened multiple times! In addition, I have to now check the cases of soda I purchase at the store as the thinner, flimsier cardboard will sometimes fall apart when being shipped.
